# Court Forms - User Guide

## For Advocates

### Creating a Court Form

1. **Open a Case**
   - Navigate to your case from the Cases list
   - Click on the case to open the case workspace

2. **Go to Court Forms Tab**
   - Click on the "Court Forms" tab in the case workspace
   - You'll see a list of any existing forms or an empty state

3. **Create New Form**
   - Click the "Create Form" button (purple button with + icon)
   - You'll see a grid of available templates

4. **Select a Template**
   - Browse through available templates:
     - Form No 45 Bail Bond
     - Vakalatnama
     - Memorandum of Appearance
     - And more...
   - Use the search box to find specific templates
   - Click on a template card to select it

5. **Edit the Form**
   - The form opens in A4 document view
   - You'll see the complete legal document with pre-written content
   - Fields that need to be filled are shown as editable boxes
   - Many fields are already filled from your case/client data
   - Click on any field to edit it
   - Type directly in the document

6. **Save Your Work**
   - Click the "Save" button (purple button at top right)
   - Your form is saved as a draft
   - You can come back and edit it anytime

7. **Share with Client**
   - Once the form is complete, click the "Share" button
   - The client will be able to view the form in their portal
   - They can review and sign it (if enabled)

### Managing Forms

#### View All Forms
- Go to the "Court Forms" tab in any case
- You'll see a list of all forms created for that case
- Each form shows:
  - Template name
  - Creation date
  - Status (Draft, Completed, Signed, Filed)
  - Signature status (Client Signed, Advocate Signed)

#### Edit a Form
- Click the eye icon on any form to view/edit it
- Make your changes
- Click "Save" to update

#### Form Status
Forms can have different statuses:
- **Draft**: Work in progress
- **Completed**: Form is complete but not yet shared
- **Shared**: Shared with client for review
- **Signed**: Signed by client and/or advocate
- **Filed**: Filed in court

### Tips for Advocates

1. **Auto-Population**: Most fields are automatically filled from case data. Review and update as needed.

2. **Save Frequently**: Click Save regularly to avoid losing your work.

3. **Professional Formatting**: The system maintains professional formatting automatically. Don't worry about spacing or alignment.

4. **Reusable Templates**: Once you fill a form, you can use it as a reference for similar cases.

5. **Client Collaboration**: Share forms with clients early for their input and approval.

## For Clients

### Viewing Court Forms

1. **Access Your Case**
   - Log in to your client portal
   - Navigate to your case

2. **Go to Court Forms Tab**
   - Click on the "Court Forms" tab
   - You'll see forms that have been shared with you

3. **Review Forms**
   - Click on any form to view it
   - The form opens in A4 document view
   - You can read the complete document
   - All fields filled by your advocate are visible

4. **Sign Forms** (if enabled)
   - Click the "Sign" button
   - Your signature is recorded with timestamp
   - The advocate is notified

5. **Download Forms** (coming soon)
   - Download PDF copies of your forms
   - Keep them for your records

### Understanding Form Status

- **Draft**: Your advocate is still working on it
- **Shared**: Ready for your review
- **Signed**: You or your advocate has signed it
- **Filed**: Submitted to court

## Common Forms Explained

### Form No 45 - Bail Bond

**What it is**: A legal document where the accused promises to appear in court on specified dates.

**When used**: When someone is granted bail in a criminal case.

**Key fields**:
- Accused name and details
- Court name and case number
- Bond amount
- Hearing dates
- FIR number

### Vakalatnama

**What it is**: A power of attorney that authorizes your advocate to represent you in court.

**When used**: At the beginning of any court case.

**Key fields**:
- Your name and address
- Advocate's name and enrollment number
- Case details
- Court name

### Memorandum of Appearance

**What it is**: A formal notice to the court that an advocate is appearing on behalf of a party.

**When used**: When an advocate enters appearance in a case.

**Key fields**:
- Advocate details
- Party being represented (Petitioner/Respondent)
- Case details
- Address for service

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I edit a form after it's shared?
Yes, advocates can edit forms even after sharing. Clients will see the updated version.

### What happens when I sign a form?
Your signature is recorded with a timestamp. The form status changes to "Signed" and the advocate is notified.

### Can I download forms as PDF?
PDF download feature is coming soon. Currently, you can view forms in the browser.

### How do I know if a form is ready?
Check the status indicator. "Shared" means it's ready for your review. Your advocate may also notify you.

### Can I suggest changes to a form?
Yes, you can communicate with your advocate through the case chat or comments feature.

### Are my forms secure?
Yes, all forms are encrypted and only accessible to you, your advocate, and authorized personnel.

### What if I make a mistake while filling a form?
No problem! Just edit the field and save again. The system keeps track of changes.

### Can I create my own templates?
Currently, only platform administrators can create templates. Contact support if you need a custom template.

## Troubleshooting

### Form not loading
- Refresh the page
- Check your internet connection
- Clear browser cache
- Try a different browser

### Can't edit fields
- Make sure you're in edit mode
- Check if the form is locked (already filed)
- Verify you have permission to edit

### Auto-population not working
- Ensure case/client data is complete
- Contact your advocate to update case information
- Some fields may need manual entry

### Formatting looks wrong
- Try zooming to 100% in your browser
- Use a modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
- Disable browser extensions that might interfere
